The plural of moose isn't meese, it's moose.

Why?

There's two main reasons:

The word "moose" is a Native American word

Words in English all come from other languages. The word goose is German, and did this fancy thing called strong declension to change to geese as a plural. Moose is Native American and came into English thousands of years after goose, meaning the strong declension never applied.

Moose is meat

In English, meats typically don't change as plurals. In English, one would say “would you like some more salmon?” and not “would you like some more salmons?” The same concept applies to moose.

All the major dictionaries agree: The plural is moose, not meese.

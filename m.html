Our Findings

“Many people like to point out that the plural form of goose is geese, so the plural form of moose must be meese. However, the reason that the plural of goose is geese is because goose derives from an ancient germanic word undergoing strong declension, wherein oo is mutated to ee. The same thing happens with the plural forms of the words foot (feet) and tooth (teeth).
However, moose is a Native American word that was added to the English lexicon about 400 years ago, and lacks the etymological reason to be pluralized in that way.”
-Hannah, Writer for the Monthly Moose

About Us

Since our founding in 2019, we've maintained one goal: educate people on the correct plural of moose.

“We've been trying to explain to people that the correct plural of moose is moose, not the commonly used but incorrect 'meese,'” explained our founder, Jonah.

After seeing his friends mispluralize moose, Jonah did some research to prove that the plural of moose is moose, built a website, and then made anti-meese pins to spread awareness. Soon after, the Monthly Moose (formerly the Moose Gazette) was founded. It was the leading publication discussing moose-related news around the world and was published for a little over a year. More recently, we've built a Discord bot that deletes the word meese (along with ton of other features). There's anti-meese shirts, stickers, and more anti-meese merch available here. Meesers are still a major sociatal issue today, and we're working to fix that.
